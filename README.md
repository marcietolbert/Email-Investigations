# Email Investigations

## Overview

This repository contains a collection of manual email analysis reports focused on identifying and evaluating potential phishing and malicious email activity. Each report demonstrates a structured approach to analyzing email artifacts, including sender domains, message headers, authentication results (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), and embedded content such as links or attachments. The goal is to determine the legitimacy of each message through careful inspection and correlation of technical and contextual indicators.

These analyses are performed without relying solely on automated tools, emphasizing critical thinking and human judgment in detecting social engineering techniques and domain-based impersonation. This repository highlights practical investigative skills, attention to detail, and the ability to assess threats in real-world scenarios where automated detection may be limited or inconclusive.

### Tools and Resources Used

- **MXToolbox** – Header analysis and email authentication checks (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

- **WHOIS Lookup / ICANN Lookup** – Domain registration and age verification

- **VirusTotal** – Domain and IP reputation analysis

- **AbuseIPDB** – IP reputation and abuse history

- **AlienVault OTX** – Threat intelligence and indicator correlation

- **URLScan.io** – URL behavior and webpage analysis (when applicable)

### Skills Learned

Through these manual email analysis exercises, the following skills were developed and strengthened:

- **Phishing Detection & Analysis** – Identifying common and advanced phishing techniques, including brand impersonation, social engineering, and deceptive messaging

- **Email Header Analysis** – Interpreting SPF, DKIM, and DMARC results to assess sender authenticity and detect spoofing attempts

- **Domain & Infrastructure Analysis** – Evaluating domain age, registrar information, and naming patterns (e.g., typosquatting, hyphenation) to identify suspicious infrastructure

- **Threat Intelligence Correlation** – Leveraging multiple data sources to validate findings and assess domain/IP reputation

- **Social Engineering Awareness** – Recognizing psychological tactics such as urgency, authority, and collaboration used to manipulate recipients

- **Attention to Detail** – Detecting subtle inconsistencies in sender information, content, and technical indicators

- **Analytical Thinking & Problem Solving** – Forming evidence-based conclusions by correlating technical and contextual data

- **Technical Documentation & Reporting** – Producing clear, structured reports that communicate findings, risk, and supporting evidence
